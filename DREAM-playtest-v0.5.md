# Operation Watchman — A Dream of the 90 Days

*A grounded playtest, written in the first person as if I were the user living
v0.5 from Day 0 to graduation.*

> **What this is, honestly.** I can't hold an iPhone, so I didn't "use" the app
> the way you will. Instead I ran the real app's logic — the date math, the
> streak rules, the confession-anchor lookup, the reading-fallback behavior,
> the graduation trigger — against the real `watchman-90.json`, over a simulated
> 90-day run starting **Monday, June 29, 2026**. Then I wrote down what that run
> would actually put in front of a man each day, and how it would *feel*. Every
> verse, every anchor, every blank prompt below is what the code genuinely
> produces. The feelings are imagined; the facts are not. That's the "dream":
> an honest simulation I can record, not a magic trick.

---

## Day 0 — The Covenant

I open it the first time and it doesn't dump me into a checklist. Good. It
stops me. Ezekiel 3:17 — *"I have stationed you as a watchman."* Then a
paragraph I didn't expect from an app: *these disciplines do not earn you
anything from God. Christ has already earned it. The streak is not your
standing.* That one line does more work than the developer probably knows. It
pulls the fishhook out before I've even started — the works-righteousness reflex
that every Reformed man feels the second he sees a checkbox next to "fasting."

It asks for my anchor prayer. I sit there longer than I expected to. That blank
box is the most serious thing on the screen. I write:

> *Lord, over these 90 days — my marriage, and the men You've put in my charge.
> Make me a man worth following before You make me a man worth listening to.*

I tap **Begin Day 1 — Stand the Post.** It's mine now.

## Week One — The app is preaching, and it's good

**Day 1 · Monday.** The week's anchor sits at the top: **LBCF 1689, Ch. 1 — Of
the Holy Scriptures.** Then five readings, and every one of them is *real* — not
a category, a passage:

- *Morning Wisdom* — Proverbs 1, "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge."
- *Husband's Post* — Ephesians 5:22–33, "Love her as Christ loved the church."
- *Father's Charge* — Deuteronomy 6:1–9, "Teach them diligently to your children."
- *Citizen's Stand* — Romans 12, "Be not conformed to this world."
- *Evening Peace* — Psalm 1.

The reflection prompt at night: *"What post has God assigned you today? What does
it mean to stand there?"* I open the journal and my anchor is sitting right there
above the cursor. I write against it. This is the loop working exactly as
designed.

**Day 3 · Wednesday.** Fasting day, and the prompt knows it: *"What did the fast
show you that fullness was hiding?"* Colossians, Psalm 78, "Be still and know
that I am God." I went to bed having actually *thought* about something.

**Day 5 · Friday.** *"If your son were watching you this week, what would he
learn about being a man?"* I felt that one in my chest. Song of Solomon for the
Husband's Post — they're not afraid of the marriage bed here, and they shouldn't
be.

**Day 7 · Sunday — ✟ The Lord's Day.** The banner changes. Gold band across the
top: *Corporate worship · Family worship · Holy rest.* It's a small thing and
it's a *huge* thing. The app stops being a taskmaster for one day and points me
to the assembly instead of to itself. Joshua 24:15, "As for me and my house."
Psalm 139, "Search me, O God." End of week one. The prompt: *"What has God done
this week that you almost missed?"*

Seven for seven. Streak says 7. I feel like I'm being formed.

And then —

## Day 8 — The cliff

**Monday, July 6.** I open the app the way I have for a week, expecting the next
move in the campaign. The anchor at top now reads **Ch. 2 — Of God and the Holy
Trinity.** Promising. But underneath:

- *Morning Wisdom* — Proverbs 8. (Good.)
- *Husband's Post* — **"Ephesians · Marriage & love passages."**
- *Father's Charge* — **"Deuteronomy · Legacy & instruction."**
- *Citizen's Stand* — **"Romans · Civic duty & righteousness."**
- *Evening Peace* — **"Reflection · Gratitude · Rest."**

Four of my five readings just turned from a *passage* into a *category*. The app
stopped telling me what to read and started telling me what *genre* to go find on
my own. And that night, the reflection prompt — the thing that made me think for
seven days straight — is **blank.** The journal opens to my anchor and a cursor
and nothing to push against.

I want to be honest about what this does to a man on Day 8. Week one earned my
trust completely. Day 8 spends it. It doesn't feel like "more is coming" — it
feels like *the staff left and handed me a worksheet template.* The
Morning Wisdom track (Proverbs, straight through) carries on faithfully to Day
90. But it's carrying the other four on its back the whole way.

**This is the single most important finding in the dream, and the replay backs
it to the decimal:**

| Reading slot | Days with a real passage |
|---|---|
| Morning Wisdom | **90 / 90** |
| Husband's Post | 7 / 90 |
| Father's Charge | 7 / 90 |
| Citizen's Stand | 7 / 90 |
| Evening Peace | 7 / 90 |

83 of 90 days have at least one placeholder slot. **332 of 450** reading-slots
across the program are category-fallbacks. **83 of 90** nights have no written
reflection prompt. The "Reformed Exodus 90" promise is fully kept for one week
and then leans entirely on Proverbs for twelve more.

## The long middle — what the dream felt like with the gap in it

**Day 14 · Sunday.** Lord's Day banner again — that rhythm never breaks, and it's
the quiet hero of the whole app. But I had a hard week. Traveled Tuesday, no cold
shower in the hotel, and I'd had a beer at the dinner I couldn't get out of. So
Tuesday wasn't all 11 checks. Which means **my streak is zero.** Not "paused."
Not "11 of 14 days." Zero. I was at 8 and one imperfect travel day sent the
counter back to the floor.

I need to flag this hard, because it's the second-biggest thing in the dream:
**the streak is all-or-nothing across 11 daily disciplines, and a single miss on
any one of them resets it to 0.** Over 90 days, the odds that a real man with a
job and a family checks all 11 boxes all 90 days are essentially nil. The streak
isn't a motivator at that point — it's a guarantee of failure that resets to
mock him. Exodus 90 men keep going after a stumble *because the fraternity holds
them*, not because the counter forgave them. We have the brittleness without the
brotherhood.

**Day 30 · Tuesday.** **Ch. 11 — Of Justification.** I laughed, a little, at the
mercy of the scheduling: the week I'm most tempted to count my performance is the
week the app anchors me in *the righteousness reckoned to me by faith, not
infused.* The weekly LBCF anchor is genuinely the best-kept promise after the
Lord's Day banner. Even on a fallback-heavy day, that one card is doing real
catechetical work. Whoever wrote those 13 summaries wrote them in the right
voice.

**Day 45 · Wednesday. Ch. 14 — Of Saving Faith.** Morning Wisdom says Proverbs
14 — which I read on Day 14. The Proverbs cycle has come back around (it runs
1–31 three times). It doesn't feel stale exactly — Proverbs rewards re-reading —
but I notice it, and on a day when the other four slots are categories, the
repeat underlines that I'm essentially doing a Proverbs-a-day plan with extra
steps for 83 of the 90 days.

**Day 62 · Saturday. Ch. 17 — Of the Perseverance of the Saints.** The right
word at the two-thirds mark, where most men quit. *"He who began a good work will
finish it."* If only the journal prompt below it weren't blank — this is the most
natural night in the entire program to ask a man *"Where have you wanted to quit,
and what kept you?"* and the app says nothing.

---

## Day 84 — Lord's Day, and the Confession lands its best blow

**Sunday, September 20. Ch. 22 — Of Religious Worship and the Sabbath Day** — on
a Sunday. The weekly anchor and the Lord's Day banner agree with each other for
once, and the effect is real: the app is teaching the Sabbath *on* the Sabbath.
That's the kind of alignment that feels designed even though it's just the
calendar and the schedule meeting. Nobody had to code it; it emerges. Lean into
that.

---

## Day 90 — Hold the line

**Saturday, September 26.** Morning Wisdom: Proverbs 28, and the one bespoke note
left in the back half of the file — *"Day 90. Stand your post. Hold the line."*
The other four slots are still categories. The last night's reflection prompt is
blank. The man who most deserves a real closing question gets an empty box.

---

## Day 91 — Graduation

**Sunday, September 27.** I open it and the checklist is gone. In its place:

> **Watchman, you have stood your post.**
>
> Ninety days. Five readings, six disciplines, the LORD's mercy daily new. The
> work is not finished — the post is permanent. What was disciplined for a season
> now becomes a way of life. Husband, father, citizen, watchman: go and do
> likewise.
>
> *I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith.
> — 2 Timothy 4:7*

Three stats: days completed, best streak, journal entries. Three buttons: Begin
Again, Other Plans, Export Journal. I export, and ninety days of my own
handwriting comes down as a markdown file with my anchor and the dates. *That* is
the artifact I keep. That's the thing I'd show another man and say "do this."

It's a genuinely good ending. It earns its verse. (One small dream-glitch worth a
note: Day 91 is itself a Sunday, so the Lord's Day banner and the graduation
screen both want the top of the page at once — harmless, but someone should
decide which wins.)

## Waking — what the dream is telling you to build

Ranked by how hard each one hit during the 90 days:

1. **Fill the back 83 days. This is the whole ballgame.** The app makes a
   covenant in week one — five real readings, a real nightly question — and then
   keeps one-fifth of it for the remaining twelve weeks. Everything else in v0.5
   is good enough that *this* gap is now the only thing standing between
   "promising shell" and "the real thing." It's a writing project, not a code
   project: ~332 reading references + 83 reflection prompts. The Husband /
   Father / Citizen / Peace tracks each need a 90-day arc.

2. **Fix the streak before a real man ever sees it.** All-or-nothing across 11
   items, resetting to 0 on a single miss, will break every honest user inside
   two weeks and then mean nothing. Options, cheapest first: (a) count "days you
   showed up" (≥1 check) separately from "perfect days"; (b) allow a grace/"liberty"
   day per week — which is *theologically on-brand* (LBCF 21, Christian liberty);
   (c) make the streak forgiving — longest run, not current-must-be-unbroken.
   My vote: a weekly grace day, named like the rest of the app.

3. **Write the 83 missing reflection prompts** *(part of #1, but call it out).*
   The nightly question is the best feature in week one and absent for 89% of the
   program. Day 62 (Perseverance) and Day 90 (the end) are the two most criminal
   blanks.

4. **Fraternity, even a fake one.** The streak is brittle *because* there's no
   brotherhood to catch a stumble. Even a v0.6-cheap "copy my check-in to the
   group chat" button changes the psychology from "the app caught me failing" to
   "my men know where I stand."

5. **The two quiet heroes — protect them.** The **Lord's Day banner** and the
   **weekly LBCF anchor** are the most distinctively *Reformed* things in the
   whole product and they cost almost nothing. Don't let future redesigns bury
   them. The day the Sabbath chapter landed on a Sunday (Day 84) is the app at
   its best.

6. **Small polish:** decide whether graduation or the Lord's Day banner owns Day
   91 when they collide; consider a closing note on each *Sunday* ("you've stood
   X weeks"); and the Proverbs cycle repeating at Day 32/63 is fine but is extra
   reason the *other four* tracks need real content — right now Proverbs is doing
   all the heavy lifting twice over.

If I only got to do one thing with you next: **#1.** The bones are good. The app
needs its body.
